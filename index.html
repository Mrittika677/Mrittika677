Agriculture In-Situ Monitor (AIM)

The Agriculture In-Situ Monitor (AIM) is a product developed by Harit Vikas Technologies. AIM is a sensor-based system designed to monitor various environmental parameters crucial for agriculture, with the ultimate goal of enhancing sustainability and transparency in carbon management. The AIM sends data to the cloud using various telemetry options. This data is visulized in CRAVE.

 

The module collects real-time environmental data such as soil pH level, NPK composition, temperature, humidity, electrical conductivity, and geographical position of the sensor.

 

Data collected by AIM is processed using a Raspberry Pi board for further analysis. Processed data is then transmitted via Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or LTE SIM modules. The data is stored remotely in Google Firestore for accessibility through APIs. AIM is expandable with modular sensors and integrates with CRAVE for a comprehensive monitoring dashboard combining Earth observation imagery for enhanced environmental insights.

 

Carbon Robust Administration and Visualization Environment (CRAVE)

CRAVE, or Carbon Robust Administration and Visualization Environment, is a web-based platform developed by Harit Vikas Technologies to monitor and analyze environmental conditions in a particular region. The goal of CRAVE is to facilitate carbon management by integrating data from Earth observation systems with in-situ data collected by sensors, such as those from their AIM (Agriculture In-Situ Monitor) system. This digital platform for carbon economy is a user-friendly, easy-accessible, and transparent platform, that provides users with the tools and information they need to take meaningful action on climate change.

 

CRAVE integrates satellite-based Earth observations with real-time sensor data from the AIM system, providing insights into environmental parameters like vegetation index, soil quality, air quality, and biomass estimation.

 

This tool is crucial for analyzing carbon sequestration in different areas and contributes to carbon management by monitoring the environmental impact of certain activities. It is also a part of Project Saaya, an open-source initiative aimed at promoting climate action through collaboration with universities and stakeholders.
